Plot Summary

Candide falls in love with Baron’s daughter, Cunégonde. When the Baron catches them kissing, Candide gets kicked out and has to fare for himself for the first time in his life. He quickly gets conscripted into the Buglar army. After being flogged as a deserter, and witnessing a horrible battle, Candide manages to escape to Holland. In Holland, James, a kind Anabaptist, takes Candide and Candide’s tutor, Pangloss, to Lisbon. During the journey to Lisbon, a storm blew a sailor to the edge of the ship so James the Anabaptist saved him but in turn caused him to fall into the sea; however, no one bothered to save him. An Inquisition-controlled Lisbon finds Candide and Pangloss guilty of being heretics, and Candide is flogged. However, he is saved by an old woman who has only one buttock because she was formerly a princess who was a captured and enslaved by men who were famished and decided that the behinds of women should be sacrificed for their hunger. When he discovers Cunégonde alive as a sex slave, he kills the two men holding her captive and they run away together to South America. When they arrive, Cunégonde leaves Candide for a governor who offers her a proposal of marriage. Upon seeing Cunégonde’s brother, he still insists on asking for Cunegonde’s hand in marriage, keeping his promise; however, when Cunégonde’s brother refuses, Candide is willing to sacrifice his life to a duel in order to win her hand. Candide is scammed as he makes his long journey back to Venice where he hears news of Cunegonde. Eventually he finds Cunégonde’s brother, now a slave, who tells them that Cunégonde, now ugly, is in Constantinople. Candide buys the freedom of the old woman, Cunégonde, a maid, Pangloss, and Cunégonde’s brother. Although Cunégonde is no longer youthful and pretty, Candide keeps his promise and marries her despite his reluctance. With the little money he has left, Candide buys a farm and allows his companions to live on his land and they cultivate a garden and become fulfilled and happy working together.
